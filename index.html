Biography

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the University of Barcelona, where I studied from 2018 to 2022. I then continued my academic journey at the same institution, completing a Master's degree in Advanced Mathematics in July 2023. Currently, I am pursuing a PhD at the University of Barcelona, which I began in October 2024. My research is centered on Dynamical Systems, with a specific focus on the formation and characterization of Strange Attractors.

Publications

  • Martinez, R. (2024, January). The Gromov-Hausdorff distance between compact metric spaces. Reports@SCM, Vol. 8. (num. 1), pag. 59-60. https://revistes.iec.cat/index.php/reports/index

    This paper provides an introduction to the Gromov-Hausdorff distance, discussing its original definition and its relation to correspondences between spaces. We demonstrate that the Gromov-Hausdorff distance serves as a metric for the set of isometry classes of compact metric spaces. The main objectives of this study are to establish the existence of a pseudometric on the disjoint union of X with Y that achieves the Gromov-Hausdorff distance between compact spaces X and Y, and to establish bounds for the Gromov-Hausdorff distance between spheres of different dimensions.
